About me

I am a recent Computer Science and Mathematics graduate from Harvey Mudd College. In August 2020, I will join the Algorithms Subgroup of Nile's Pierce's lab at Caltech. There I will be moving NUPACK, a software suite for the design and analysis of nucleic acids, to the cloud.

In addition to developing applications, I also enjoy sharing my knowledge with others. During my time at Harvey Mudd, I tutored my peers in both computer science and mathematics classes. I also frequently tutor middle and high school students.

Experience & Education

  • Schmidt Scholar in Software Engineering
    California Institute of Technology
    August 2020

    NUPACK is a software system for design and analysis of nucleic acid structures, devices and systems. The current 240-core hosting hardware is frequently overwhelmed as the worldwide usage grows. As a part of the Algorithms subgroup of Niles Pierce's lab, I will work to move NUPACK from its current static hardware to a dynamic cloud-hosted system that can grow and shrink with demand.

  • Computer Science Clinic with Paramount Animation
    Harvey Mudd College
    September 2019 - May 2020

    For my senior capstone, I worked on a team of 5 undergraduate students to develop an asset brower for Paramount Animation. During film production, Paramount Animation produces a large volume of assets such as concept art, 3D models, sets, and more. Individual assets often require attention from a variety of personnel throughout the animation pipeline. An asset browser is a tool that intelligently navigates these animation assets. My team and I met weekly with our liasions from Paramount and conducted user experience research to design an asset browser for feature film development that accomodates Paramount Animation's worflow. After the design process was complete, we built a prototype of our design using Qt and MongoDB.

    For our work on this project, my team was awarded a Clinic Team Award by the computer science faculty.
